Conlang Statuses

There are a total of 7 conlang statuses, and they represent stages of development.
Their order in completedness are;

  • Discontinued - indicates that a conlang was completely given up, and there's no reason / intention to continue working on that conlang.
  • Planning - indicates that a conlang is currently being planned, and only basic features are done.
  • In Development - indicates that a conlang is currently actually being considered and is undergoing major development.
  • Shaping - indicates that a conlang is currently actively being shaped, and core grammar features are done.
  • Final Development - indicates that a conlang is almost completed, being reviewed constantly, and getting new advanced vocabulary.
  • Completed - indicates that a conlang has been completed, and fixes will be minimal unless urgent.
  • Archived - indicates that a conlang has been inspected for long enough, and only minimal fixes are available.

Part of Speech Abbreviations

Types of Words in a Sentence

I usually use these abbreviations for clarifying the use of words in conlang dictionaries.

  • [n] - noun
  • [v] - verb
  • [adj] - adjective
  • [adv] - adverb (specific)
  • [pro] - pronoun
  • [det] - determiner
  • [prep] - preposition
  • [con] - conjunction
  • [int] - interjection, phrase
  • [part] - particle, can also mean any kind of article / marker
