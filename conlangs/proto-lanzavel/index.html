Proto-Lanzavel

Welcome to my conlang! This is Proto-Lanzavel, which is a more ancient version of the modern Lanzavel language.
This is not a document for those who wants to study the language. It's a basic foundation of the conlang.
Once this language gets out of the development status, it will be adjusted to new learners. But for now, there may be mistakes, inconsistencies, and variable information.
Lanzavel is supposed to be a minimalist conlang that can also somewhat convey meaning.
It's similar in structure as of in Japanese since it's a pro-drop and SOV language, but uses the Latin script.

Vowel Clusters

A vowel cluster is defined as two vowels together in the same syllable.
Only up to two vowels are considered a vowel cluster.
Three or more vowels in a vowel cluster are deemed new syllables, and the separator must be used for those cases.
Vowel clusters are pronounced very straight-forwardly. No vowel cluster makes an irregular pronunciation, except for the examples below;

Vowel Cluster IPA Pronunciation(s) Information
ae [æ]
oe [œ]

The separator (') can be used to separate a vowel cluster, and must be used when three or more vowels are in a single vowel cluster.
Here is an example;

kael (/ˈkʰæl/), ka'el (/ˈkʰaˌ.el/)

Syllable Structure

The syllable structure is usually the below in most cases;

(C)(C)(L)(L)(V)V(C)(C)

Where;

  • [C] is any consonant except Jj and Ww
  • [L] is Jj or Ww but not simultaneously both
  • [V] is any vowel

Capitalization, Punctuation and more

Capitalization is usually not mandatory in informal contexts, just like in most languages.
Capitalization is only used for the contexts below;

  • Human names (for example, Kousei Lanso, Haisei Kjomeikon)
  • Brand names (for example, Diskord, Vaselin)
  • Location names (for example, Paris, Frans)
    By the way, you say "a person from country [N]" [N] pekra (lit. [N] person => person from [N])
  • Start of every sentence (for example, Mi en Lanso.)
  • Every word in a title, except for particles and articles

Punctuation is usually pretty similar to English, and there's not much that I can say here.
